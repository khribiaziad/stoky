Stocky
Stock, Order & Finance Management for Moroccan E-Commerce
Type: Internal Operations Tool Market: Morocco (MAD, Arabic carrier PDFs) Stack: React + FastAPI + SQLite

1 What is Stocky?

Stocky is an all-in-one back-office tool for small Moroccan e-commerce businesses that sell physical products through shipping carriers (primarily Caleo / Amana / similar). It replaces scattered spreadsheets by centralising stock, orders, team management and financial reporting into one dark-themed web app.

Core problem it solves: Merchants receive PDF manifests from carriers (pickup & return lists). Manually cross-referencing these against stock, calculating profit per order, and tracking delivery rates is time-consuming and error-prone. Stocky automates the entire loop.

Who uses it?

  • Admin (store owner) — full access, sees all finances and reports
  • Confirmer (team member) — uploads pickup PDFs, processes returns; cannot see finances or modify products/stock

4 Key Concepts & Terminology

CMD-ID (Caleo ID) The unique parcel tracking number printed on the carrier pickup PDF. Used to match returns automatically.
Variant A specific size + color combination of a product. Stock and pricing are tracked at the variant level.
Pack A bundle product sold as a unit (e.g. "3-cap pack"). Composed of multiple variants with optional preset combinations.
Sell Bag Per-order packaging expense (1 MAD). Auto-checked if any product in the order is flagged as under 1kg.
Delivery Fee Per-city fee paid to the carrier on delivery. Looked up from the city list, falls back to a configurable default.
Return Fee Fee charged by the carrier when a parcel is returned. Deducted from profit on cancelled orders.
Clean Profit Revenue − COGS − delivery fees − packaging − team costs − fixed expenses − ad spend. The bottom-line number.
Capital Cash balance + stock value at cost. Gives the owner a real-time net worth of the business.
Confirmer A team member role with a limited sub-account. Can upload pickup/return PDFs and view their own stats only.
Broken Stock Units damaged in returns or storage. Logged separately; value lost is tracked for accurate profit calculation.

6 Current UX Notes & Friction Points

Manual product assignment after PDF upload — The biggest daily friction point. After uploading a pickup PDF with 30+ orders, the user must assign a product variant to each order one by one. A smart default (e.g. "last used product" or quick-assign all to one product) would help.
City name matching is fuzzy but not perfect — Delivery fees are looked up by city name. If the carrier writes "Casablanca" differently across PDFs (e.g. "Casa", "CASABLANCA"), it may fall back to the default fee. The is_casa flag helps for same-city orders but city name normalisation is manual.
No mobile-native experience — The app is responsive (bottom nav on mobile) but the order assignment modal with large dropdowns is difficult on small screens. Confirmers who use phones in the field would benefit from a simplified mobile view.
PDF parsing reliability — Pickup and return PDF parsing depends on pdfplumber table extraction. If a carrier changes their PDF format, parsing fails silently or produces wrong data. A preview/validation step before orders are created would reduce errors.
Strengths: The financial model is solid and fully automatic once orders are tagged. The city fee system (with PDF import for carrier price lists) is a real time-saver. The confirmer sub-account model cleanly separates responsibilities without requiring a separate app.

7 High-Level Data Flow

  CARRIER PDF (pickup)
        │
        ▼
  [Orders Router]  ←── parse_pickup_pdf()  ←── pdfplumber
        │
        ├─ creates Order records (status = pending)
        ├─ creates OrderItem records
        ├─ deducts Variant.stock
        └─ creates OrderExpense (delivery_fee from City table)
                                │
                                │  city not found?
                                └──→ fallback fee from AppSettings

  CARRIER PDF (returns)
        │
        ▼
  [Orders Router]  ←── parse_return_pdf()
        │
        ├─ matches CMD-IDs → existing Orders
        ├─ sets status = cancelled
        ├─ restores Variant.stock  (if not broken)
        └─ creates BrokenStock record (if broken)

  REPORTS
        │
        ├─ reads Orders (delivered only for revenue)
        ├─ reads OrderExpenses (delivery + return fees)
        ├─ reads OrderItems × buying_price (COGS)
        ├─ reads FixedExpenses (prorated by period)
        ├─ reads FacebookAds (daily_rate × days × usd_rate)
        ├─ reads TeamMember (monthly salary + per_order × delivered)
        └─ outputs: revenue, gross_profit, clean_profit, capital
